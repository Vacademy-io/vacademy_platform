Chapter 1

Getting Around the App

Everything in the app is reachable from two places: your dashboard (the home screen) and the main navigation menu.

  1. 1Sign in with the credentials shared by your institute. If you forget your password, use the "Forgot password" link on the login screen to reset it.
  2. 2After signing in you land on the Dashboard. It shows what needs your attention first: continue-learning shortcuts, upcoming schedules, and your recent activity.
  3. 3Open the navigation menu (the sidebar on a computer, or the menu button in the corner on a phone). From here you can reach your courses, assessments, live class and reports.
  4. 4The bell icon in the top bar shows notifications from your institute. A number badge means something new is waiting for you.
  5. 5Your profile menu (top-right corner) lets you view your details, change your password, and sign out.
  6. 6The Help button (question-mark icon) in the top bar replays interactive tutorials for any of these features whenever you need a refresher.
Tips
  • Add the app to your phone home screen (or install the mobile app if your institute provides one) so you are one tap away from class.
  • Allow notifications when the app asks — schedule changes and announcements arrive there first.
Chapter 2

Browsing and Opening Courses

Your learning library holds every course you are enrolled in, organised so you can always pick up where you left off.

  1. 1Open Learning Center from the navigation menu to see all your courses. Each card shows the course name, cover image and your progress so far.
  2. 2Tap a course card to open it. The outline view lists its subjects and modules in the order you are meant to study them.
  3. 3Expand a module to see its chapters. A chapter groups related lessons together.
  4. 4Open any chapter to see its slides — videos, documents, quizzes and assignments appear in sequence.
  5. 5Use the progress bar on each course card to see how much you have completed. Progress updates automatically as you study.
  6. 6To return to a course later, use the "Continue learning" section on your dashboard — it takes you straight to your last position.
Tips
  • New courses appear automatically when your institute enrols you — there is nothing to activate.
  • If a card looks locked, the content may be released on a schedule. Check back on the release date or ask your teacher.
Chapter 3

Watching Videos and Studying Slides

Lessons are delivered as slides: videos, readings, quizzes and practice activities that you work through in order.

  1. 1Open a chapter and tap the first slide to start studying. The viewer opens with the lesson content front and center.
  2. 2For videos, use the player controls to pause, rewind ten seconds, change playback speed, or go full screen.
  3. 3Move between slides with the next/previous arrows, or jump to any slide from the list at the side.
  4. 4A checkmark appears on each slide you complete. Videos count as complete when you have watched them through.
  5. 5Some lessons include quick quizzes. Answer each question to continue — they help you check your understanding as you go.
  6. 6If you have a question about a lesson, use the doubt/help option in the viewer to ask your teacher directly from that lesson.
  7. 7Your watch time and completion are saved automatically, even if you close the app mid-lesson.
Tips
  • Studying on a slow connection? Lower the video quality from the player settings for smoother playback.
  • Short, regular study sessions beat marathon ones — the app tracks your daily streak to help you stay consistent.
Chapter 4

Taking an Assessment

Assessments are timed tests assigned by your institute. Everything from starting an attempt to reviewing results happens in the app.

  1. 1Open Assessments from the navigation menu. Tests are grouped by status: live (open now), upcoming, and past.
  2. 2Tap an assessment to see its details — subject, number of questions, total marks, duration and the attempt window.
  3. 3Read the instructions carefully, then tap Start when you are ready. The timer begins immediately and keeps running even if you close the app.
  4. 4Answer questions using the on-screen options. Use the question palette to jump between questions and see which ones are answered, skipped or marked for review.
  5. 5Mark tricky questions for review and come back to them before time runs out.
  6. 6Tap Submit when you are done. If the timer expires first, your attempt is submitted automatically with the answers you have saved.
  7. 7After results are released, open the assessment again (or go to Reports) to see your score, correct answers and explanations.
Tips
  • Join a few minutes early with a charged device and stable internet — the timer does not pause for connection problems.
  • Do not switch apps during a proctored test; it may be flagged or end your attempt, depending on your institute’s rules.
Chapter 5

Joining a Live Class

Live Class are scheduled sessions taught in real time. The app shows you the schedule and gets you into the room in one tap.

  1. 1Open Live Class from the navigation menu (under Learning Center). Today's sessions appear first, followed by the upcoming schedule.
  2. 2Each card shows the topic, teacher, start time and duration, so you know exactly what is coming up.
  3. 3When a session is live, its card shows a Join button. Tap it and the class opens right in the app.
  4. 4Allow microphone (and camera, if asked) permissions the first time — your teacher controls when participants can speak.
  5. 5Use the in-class chat to ask questions, and the raise-hand option if your teacher has enabled it.
  6. 6If you get disconnected, come back to the same card and tap Join again — you will re-enter the ongoing session.
  7. 7Missed a class? Recordings appear in the same place after the session, whenever your institute shares them.
Tips
  • Turn on notifications to get a reminder before each session starts.
  • Headphones noticeably improve audio quality in live sessions.
Chapter 6

Tracking Your Learning Progress

The app keeps a running record of everything you study and every test you take, so you always know where you stand.

  1. 1Open Reports (under Assessments) to see every assessment you have attempted, with marks, percentile and rank where available.
  2. 2Tap any attempt to open the detailed report — question-by-question review with correct answers and explanations once results are released.
  3. 3Your dashboard's analytics widgets chart daily study time, courses completion and activity trends across the week.
  4. 4Daily streaks track how consistently you study. Keep the streak alive by completing at least one activity a day.
  5. 5Each course's progress bar shows completion; open the course to see exactly which chapters remain.
  6. 6Certificates (when your institute enables them) unlock automatically once you cross the completion threshold — look for the certificate option inside the completed course.
Tips
  • Review wrong answers within a day or two of getting results — that is when the review sticks best.
  • A few minutes of progress-checking every week helps you catch weak areas before exams do.
