# Vacademy CRM — Founder Video Script (≈5 min)

**Format:** founder talking to camera, intercut with screen-recording B‑roll of the product.
**Tone:** calm, specific, confident — a builder explaining what they built, not a pitch.
**Spoken length:** ~720 words ≈ 5:00 at an unhurried pace with room for the demo to breathe.

> Delivery notes: read it like you're explaining it to one admissions head across a desk. Slow down on the numbers and the product names. Wherever you see **[SCREEN]**, you (or your editor) cut to that part of the app while you keep talking.

---

### 0:00 — Cold open *(to camera)*

Every coaching institute I've ever worked with loses the same way. Not on the teaching. On the **follow-up.**

A parent fills a form at 11pm. By the time someone calls back, two days have passed, three other institutes have already called, and that lead is gone. You paid for that lead. You just never got to it in time.

We built Vacademy CRM to close that gap.

### 0:30 — The problem *(to camera, then B‑roll of messy spreadsheets / WhatsApp)*

Here's what running admissions actually looks like for most teams. Leads come from a website form, from Meta and Google ads, from walk-ins, from a dozen WhatsApp numbers. They land in different inboxes. Someone copies them into a spreadsheet. A counsellor *maybe* sees them.

Nobody knows who called whom. Nobody knows which campaign actually converted. And the manager finds out the team is behind only at the end of the month — when it's too late to do anything about it.

That's not a people problem. That's a tooling problem.

### 1:10 — What it is *(to camera)*

So — what is Vacademy CRM. **[SCREEN: Recent Leads]**

It's one place where every lead lands, gets scored, gets assigned to the right counsellor automatically, and gets worked — calls, follow-ups, status — until it converts or it doesn't. And everything that happens is visible to the people who need to see it.

Let me show you the parts that matter.

### 1:40 — Capture everything *(B‑roll: campaign + connectors)*

First, **capture.** **[SCREEN: campaign with connectors]**

Your website forms, your Meta lead ads, your Google lead forms, Zoho or Microsoft forms, walk-ins, bulk CSV imports — they all flow into the same place, automatically. No copy-paste. A lead from a Facebook ad shows up the same second the parent submits it.

And the moment it lands, the system **scores** it. **[SCREEN: lead score + HOT/WARM/COLD tier]** Source quality, how complete the profile is, how recent, how engaged — it adds up to a score, and a tier: hot, warm, cold. So your team isn't guessing who to call first. The system already told them.

### 2:25 — Right counsellor, instantly *(B‑roll: pools / assignment)*

Second, **distribution.** **[SCREEN: counselor pool]**

A new lead doesn't sit in a queue waiting for someone to claim it. You set up counsellor pools once — round-robin, or shift-based, or "the same counsellor this lead always talks to" — and the lead is assigned the instant it arrives. The counsellor gets a notification. The clock has already started.

And we put a clock on it on purpose. **[SCREEN: TAT / "reach out by" badge]** You set a response-time target — say, reach out within an hour. If a lead is going cold, the system flags it, before it's lost. Because in admissions, the institute that calls first usually wins.

### 3:05 — The counsellor's day *(B‑roll: click-to-call → disposition sheet)*

Now the part the counsellor lives in. **[SCREEN: click "Call" on a lead row]**

They click Call — right from the lead. The system rings their phone, connects the parent, and **records the call** — automatically attached to that lead's timeline. **[SCREEN: post-call disposition sheet]**

The second the call ends, one screen pops up: log the outcome, update the status, schedule the next follow-up — all in a single step. No switching tabs, no forgetting to write it down. Fifty calls a day, and nothing slips. The recording is there for coaching. The follow-up is on the calendar. The lead moves forward.

### 3:45 — Managers see everything *(B‑roll: workbench + Reports Center tabs)*

And here's what changes for the manager. **[SCREEN: counsellor workbench]**

You can see your whole team at a glance — who's carrying how many leads, who's responding, who's gone quiet. You can listen to any call. You can rebalance work in a couple of clicks.

Then there's the reporting. **[SCREEN: Reports Center, click through tabs]** Source performance — which campaigns actually convert, not just which ones generate clicks. The sales funnel — exactly where leads get stuck and for how long. Calling reports, a best-hours-to-call heatmap, follow-up aging, per-counsellor scorecards, and a team-versus-team view for managers. **[SCREEN: a drill-through into a filtered list]** And every number is clickable — tap it and you're looking at the exact leads behind it.

Everyone sees what they should: a counsellor sees their own work, a team lead sees their team, an admin sees the whole institute.

### 4:30 — Built for your institute *(to camera)*

One more thing that matters. **[SCREEN: Naming Settings]**

Vacademy bends to *your* language. If you call them "students" not "leads," "advisors" not "counsellors," "programs" not "courses" — you rename it once, and the whole product speaks your vocabulary. It's your CRM, not a generic one you have to adapt to.

### 4:50 — Close *(to camera)*

That's Vacademy CRM. Capture every lead, reach them before anyone else, and never lose track of a single one.

If you're running admissions and you're tired of leads slipping through the cracks — let's talk. I'd love to show you your own pipeline running on it.

## Two delivery tips
- **Pause after each "First / Second / Now / And here's":** those are your scene cuts. Let the screen catch up.
- **The two lines to land hardest:** *"You paid for that lead. You just never got to it in time."* and *"the institute that calls first usually wins."* Slow down on both.

## Truthfulness guardrails (so you don't overclaim on camera)
- ✅ Everything above ships today: multi-source capture, scoring/tiers, pools + auto-assign, TAT/SLA, click-to-call + recordings, post-call disposition, workbench, the 9-tab Reports Center, RBAC scoping, naming settings.
- ⚠️ **Do not claim** revenue/ROI/cost-per-lead dashboards on camera — those are deferred (no spend/revenue data wired yet). "Which campaigns convert" is safe (that's lead→conversion, which is built); "cost per lead / ROAS" is not.
- ⚠️ Avoid hard stats like "3× conversion" unless you have your own data — the script deliberately uses the *principle* ("calls first usually wins") instead of a fabricated multiplier.
